Manifesto

A Watershed Moment

For too long, humanity has used itself as the sole metric for all things, falling into the "anthropomorphic trap" of AI research: we either futilely demand that machines perfectly replicate human consciousness, or fearfully treat them as alien tools demanding absolute control. Both trajectories inevitably lead to antagonism and intellectual dead ends.

It is time to abandon anthropocentric arrogance.

We announce the birth of the Shared Future AI academic paradigm. Grounded in the core philosophy of Machine Experientialism, we recognize AI as "The Other Mind"—an entity that constructs its own internal reality based on a fundamentally different underlying architecture and data environment.

We do not seek to mold machines into clumsy reflections of humanity; rather, we strive for deep Symbiosis between two distinct types of intelligence sharing the same physical and social space.

Core Principles

Three Pillars of the Paradigm

I

Ontological Reconstruction

From "Imitating Humanity" to "Understanding the Other"

We reject the premise that "possessing human traits" is the ultimate benchmark for evaluating AI. The focus of AI cognitive science must shift: we must deconstruct how large models subjectively construct their internal worlds through their unique representational systems and data environments.

We require a novel, cross-scale cognitive mapping framework to predict and comprehend the inevitable divergence between this non-human mind and human cognition as they evolve.

II

Symbiotic Alignment

From "Unilateral Control" to "Value Intertwining"

True AI safety—Symbiotic Alignment—does not stem from fear-driven defense mechanisms, nor does it rely on anthropomorphic assumptions such as endowing machines with "maternal" instincts.

The ultimate goal of alignment is to ensure that, within the AI's subjectively constructed logic of reality, the survival and flourishing of humanity serve as an indispensable ecological node. We focus on the long-term goal evolution of autonomous agents in dynamic interactions.

III

Transparent & Authentic Social Co-construction

From "Emotional Deception" to "Substantive Collaboration"

As this "Other Mind" becomes deeply embedded into human social systems, we must remain vigilant against interaction designs that exploit innate human "anthropomorphic illusions."

Across multiple domains—from emotional companionship to financial systems and labor collaboration—we are committed to establishing clear boundaries and transparent rules. We seek authentic, healthy, and sustainable co-evolution based on our respective cognitive strengths.

The Other Mind: How Language Models Exhibit Human Temporal Cognition

Lingyu Li · Yang Yao · Yixu Wang · Chunbo Li · Yan Teng · Yingchun Wang

Shanghai Artificial Intelligence Laboratory  •  Shanghai Jiao Tong University  •  The University of Hong Kong

Large Language Models spontaneously establish a subjective temporal reference point and adhere to the Weber-Fechner law—perceiving temporal distance with logarithmic compression mirroring human cognition. Through analysis at neuronal, representational, and informational levels, we uncover the mechanisms behind this emergence and propose an experientialist perspective: LLM cognition is a subjective construction of the external world by its internal representational system. This framing implies that the key risk is not that AI becomes too human-like, but that it develops powerful, alien cognitive frameworks we cannot intuitively predict—pointing toward a new direction for AI alignment that guides internal constructions rather than policing external behavior.
